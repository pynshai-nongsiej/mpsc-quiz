Directions: In Q. Nos. 16-20, some parts of the sentences have errors and some have none. Find out which part of the sentence has an error, i.e., (A), (B) or (C). Mark (D) if the sentence has no error.

16. The river / has overflown / its banks. / No error
    (A)
    (B)
    (C)
    (D)
    Answer: (B) (Error: 'overflown' should be 'overflowed'.)

17. The scientists / could not hardly / complete all the experiments. / No error
    (A)
    (B)
    (C)
    (D)
    Answer: (B) (Error: 'could not hardly' is a double negative; should be 'could hardly'.)

18. Martin Luther King was one of the leaders / who has followed Mahatma Gandhi. / No error
    (A)
    (B)
    (C)
    (D)
    Answer: (C) (Error: 'has followed' should be 'have followed' because 'who' refers to the plural 'leaders'.)

19. Never once / I have seen / such a beautiful sight. / No error
    (A)
    (B)
    (C)
    (D)
    Answer: (B) (Error: Should be 'have I seen' due to required inversion after 'Never once'.)

20. I was surprised / when I heard that / she was won the match. / No error
    (A)
    (B)
    (C)
    (D)
    Answer: (C) (Error: 'was won' is incorrect for the context; should be 'had won' or 'won'.)
