*** Directions: CHOOSE THE OPPOSITE MEANING (Antonyms) (Q. Nos. 1-5) ***

1. There is pandemonium in the hall.
a) confusion
b) disquiet
c) peace
d) friendship
[Answer: (c)]

2. My neighbour is an absolute snob.
a) modest
b) extrovert
c) introvert
d) pretender
[Answer: (a)]

3. It is going to be an arduous journey.
a) strenuous
b) risky
c) easy
d) enjoyable
[Answer: (c)]

4. She looked crestfallen when her friend lost the match.
a) sad
b) elated
c) depressed
d) eager
[Answer: (b)]

5. It was a macabre sight.
a) pleasant
b) horrid
c) unknown
d) comic
[Answer: (a)]

*** Directions: FIND THE ERROR (Q. Nos. 46-50) ***

46. He has purchased (a) / many furnitures (b) / from the market (c) / No error (d).
[Answer: (b)] (Error: 'furnitures' should be 'furniture' or 'many pieces of furniture')

47. I am (a)/ going foreign (b) / next month (c) / No error (d)
[Answer: (b)] (Error: 'going foreign' should be 'going abroad')

48. If you will run (a) / you will (b) / catch the train (c) / No error (d)
[Answer: (a)] (Error: 'If you will run' should be 'If you run' in a conditional sentence)

49. I wonder (a) / if ten thousand rupees (b) / are a large sum (c) / No error (d)
[Answer: (c)] (Error: 'are a large sum' should be 'is a large sum' because 'ten thousand rupees' is treated as a single amount)

50. The soup will taste better (a) / if it has (b) / more salt in it (c) / No error (d)
[Answer: (d)]
