Directions: In each of the following questions there is a capitalized word which has been used in four different way. Select the option in which the usage of the word is not appropriate.

28. RAW
a) some tribes in Africa eat raw meat.
b) it was such a raw deal
c) his style is still raw
d) he had a raw with his friends last night
**Answer: d) he had a raw with his friends last night** (The idiomatic expression is 'had a **row**,' meaning an argument)

29. CLOSE
a) if you close your eyes, you will not be able to see.
b) this road is closed.
c) Rahul and Ravi are closed friends.
d) the judge declared the case closed.
**Answer: c) Rahul and Ravi are closed friends.** (The correct term is '**close** friends')

30. PACK
a) people packed into the stadium to watch the cricket match.
b) what pack do they offer in the company ?
c) a pack of dogs hounded them.
d) the aircraft's engines packed up
**Answer: b) what pack do they offer in the company ?** (The correct term here is likely 'package' or 'pay' depending on context, not 'pack')

31. TRAVEL
a) light travels faster than sound.
b) my mind travelled over the past events
c) the railway travels hundreds of miles across the country.
d) travel helps to broaden our understanding of other people.
**Answer: c) the railway travels hundreds of miles across the country.** (A railway is a system, not the entity that 'travels'; the **train** travels, or the railway **runs** across the country)

32. MIND
a) I have made up my mind.
b) we are all of one mind on this issue.
c) would you please mind the baby while I'm gone?
d) I mind you to be careful when you are alone in the house.
**Answer: d) I mind you to be careful when you are alone in the house.** (The correct verb phrase would be 'I **remind** you' or 'I **warn** you')

33. He ______ Indian journalism to educate the public.

38.

40.

c) His house comprises three bedrooms and a toilet
d) His house consists with three bedrooms and a toilet
**Answer: c) His house comprises three bedrooms and a toilet** (The verb 'comprises' does not take a preposition; 'consists' requires 'of')
