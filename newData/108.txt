*** Directions: Choose the correct option (Q. Nos. 1-15) ***

1. India is committed ____ a policy of peaceful co-existence.
 a) Of
 b) To
 c) With
 d) For
[Answer: (b)]

2. I congratulated my classmates ____ passing the exam.
 a) For
 b) With
 c) On
 d) In
[Answer: (c)]

3. The boy, ____ father is a doctor, is my friend.
 a) Which
 b) Who
 c) Whom
 d) Whose
[Answer: (d)]

4. One must defend ____ against bullies.
 a) Herself
 b) Themselves
 c) Oneself
 d) Ourselves
[Answer: (c)]

5. If you study hard, you ____ a first class.
 a) Are getting
 b) Will get
 c) Will be getting
 d) Shall get
[Answer: (b)]

6. He ____ from malaria since the day before yesterday.
 a) Has been suffering
 b) Have been suffering
 c) Suffers
 d) is suffering
[Answer: (a)]

7. The thief and the eye-witness ____
 a) Has escaped
 b) Has been escaping
 c) Was escaping
 d) Have escaped
[Answer: (d)]

8. The police held an inquiry ____ the murder.
 a) About
 b) Into
 c) Over
 d) Across
[Answer: (b)]

9. It is due to their lethargy that the plan fell ____
 a) Through
 b) Out
 c) Away
 d) Apart
[Answer: (a)]

10. We often go fishing ____ the river bank.
 a) Towards
 b) Inside
 c) Along
 d) Around
[Answer: (c)]

11. ____ his friends speak any English.
 a) Neither of
 b) Some
 c) Both of
 d) None of these
[Answer: (a)] (Assuming the intent of the sentence is to state that zero friends speak English, as 'None of these' is too broad, and 'Neither of' is for two people, but often used informally for more than two, or the original question meant only two friends. Given the options, 'Neither of' is the most likely correct answer for a competitive exam question, potentially implying "Neither of his (two) friends...")

12. Unfortunately, I have ____ talent for music although I have always wanted to be a famous singer.
 a) A few
 b) Little
 c) Plenty of
 d) Few
[Answer: (b)] ('Little' is used for uncountable nouns like 'talent', meaning 'almost none')

13. If were you, I ____ be careful with my words.
 a) Will
 b) Would
 c) Shall
 d) Should
[Answer: (b)] (Used for hypothetical or unreal conditions: "If I were you...")

14. The mother of the dead child was overwhelmed ____ grief.
 a) By
 b) With
 c) From
 d) For
[Answer: (b)]

15. The conditions necessary ____ this project have not been met.
 a) Of completion
 b) For the complete of
 c) of complete
 d) For the completion of
[Answer: (d)]

24. a) After the picnic, we had lots of bread left.
 b) After the picnic we had lot of breads left.
 c) After the picnic we had lot of bread left.
 d) After the picnic, we had lots of breads left.
[Answer: (a)] (The plural form is **lots of bread**, not 'breads' in this context)

25. a) I did my work quick.
 b) I did my work very quick.
 c) **I did my work quickly.**
 d) None of the above
[Answer: (c)] ('Quickly' is the adverb needed to modify the verb 'did')

26. a) I am ready to say this at her face.
 b) I am ready to say this on her face.
 c) I am ready to say this in her face.
 d) **I am ready to say this to her face.**
[Answer: (d)]

27. a) **The villagers were enjoying winter evening around the fire.**
 b) The villagers were enjoying winter evening around fire.
 c) The villagers were enjoying winter evening round fire.
 d) The villagers were enjoying winter evening all round fire.
[Answer: (a)] (The use of the article 'the' before 'fire' is correct here)

28. a) The team is confident to win the match.
 b) **The team is confident of winning the match.**
 c) The team is confident to win match.
 d) The team is confident for winning the match.
[Answer: (b)] ('Confident' takes the preposition 'of', followed by a gerund 'winning')
