Each question carries 2 marks:

Directions: (Q.NO.1-5), In these questions, out of four alternatives given, choose the one which best expresses the opposite meaning of the underlined word (Antonyms).

1. He walked in ungainly strides.
a) Graceful
b) Quick
c) Short
d) Awkward
Answer: a) Graceful

2. The speaker had a superficial knowledge of the subject.
a) Artificial
b) Fictitious
c) Overt
d) Deep
Answer: d) Deep

3. This officer is noted for his zeal.
a) Indifference
b) Intolerance
c) Inefficiency
d) Immaturity
Answer: a) Indifference

4. As an officer, he was callous to his subordinate staff.
a) Courteous
b) Considerate
c) Helpful
d) Tolerant
Answer: b) Considerate

5. His transfer order was revoked yesterday.
a) Issued
b) Approved
c) Proposed
d) Prepared
Answer: a) Issued

Directions: (Q.NO.6-10), In these questions, out of four alternatives given, choose the one which best expresses the meaning of the underlined word (Synonyms).

6. We were taken aback at the fulsome praise heaped upon our former enemy.
a) Elaborated
b) Exorbitant
c) Excessive
d) Extravagant
Answer: c) Excessive

7. He gave an impromptu speech on the occasion.
a) Eloquently
b) Without preparation
c) Without enthusiasm
d) Forcefully
Answer: b) Without preparation

8. As it was her first time swimming in the sea, her apprehension was understandable.
a) Eagerness
b) Fear
c) Hesitation
d) Excitement
Answer: b) Fear

9. The perpetual noise made it impossible for the students to concentrate on their work.
a) Irritating
b) Unlimited
c) Recurrent
d) Constant
Answer: d) Constant

10. There is abundant supply of water for the crops.
a) Considerable
b) Adequate
c) Plentiful
d) Sufficient
Answer: c) Plentiful

Directions: (Q.NO.11-15), In these questions, some of the sentences have errors and some have none. Find out which part of a sentence a, b, c, d has an error and select that part as an answer. If there is no error, then 'd' is the answer.

11. Gold (a) / is (b) / an useful metal (c) / No error (d).
Answer: c) an useful metal (Error: Should be 'a useful metal')

12. The Mahatma Gandhi (a) / was also (b) / called Bapu (c) / No error (d).
Answer: a) The Mahatma Gandhi (Error: The definite article 'The' is not used before the name)

13. The Taj Mahal (a) / is a beautiful (b)/monument in Agra (c) / No error (d).
Answer: d) No error.

14. Will you please give me (a) / little milk (b)/ for my cat (c) / No error (d).
Answer: b) little milk (Error: Should be 'a little milk' to mean 'some milk')

15. My mother forbade my sister (a) / to go to theatre (b) / at night (c) / No error (d).
Answer: b) to go to theatre (Error: Should be 'to go to the theatre')

Directions: (Q.NO.16-25), In the following questions, sentences are given with blanks to be filled with appropriate and suitable word/words. Four alternatives are suggested for each question. Choose the correct alternative out of the four.

16. The employees' strike last week was a minor _______ for the company.
a) Set down
b) Set about
c) Set up
d) Set back
Answer: d) Set back

17. We still have to _______ the finer details about our upcoming trip.
a) Iron out
b) Iron in
c) Iron on
d) Iron back
Answer: a) Iron out

18. Due to financial problems, the Company had to _______ fifty employees.
a) Lay out
b) Lay off
c) Lay over
d) Lay back
Answer: b) Lay off

19. Even though he was tired, he _______.
a) Kept going
b) Kept up
c) Kept out
d) Kept in
Answer: a) Kept going

20. I keep all my _______ papers under lock and key.
a) Required
b) Necessary
c) Useful
d) Confidential
Answer: d) Confidential

21. He is very keen _______ going abroad for his studies.
a) On
b) For
c) Over
d) At
Answer: a) On

22. His approach to work is so _______ that none of his colleagues consider him dependable.
a) Low
b) Casual
c) Common
d) Uninteresting
Answer: b) Casual

23. We do not know what _______ him to commit this crime.
a) Prompted
b) Excited
c) Roused
d) Attracted
Answer: a) Prompted

24. The country needs a _______ government to tackle the challenges it faces today.
a) Stagnant
b) Stable
c) Sustained
d) Stationary
Answer: b) Stable

25. Jack felt as if he had been _______ when he lost his job.
a) Let down
b) Let on
c) Let over
d) Let up
Answer: a) Let down
