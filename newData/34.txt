Directions (Q. Nos. 1-10): Choose the one which best expresses the meaning of the underlined word out of the given alternatives (Synonyms).

1. You must ascertain the facts before you make such accusations.
   (A) Discover
   (B) Make sure of
   (C) Search
   (D) Acknowledge
   Answer: (B) Make sure of

2. The minister's speech seeks to allay the fears of the people.
   (A) Control
   (B) Heighten
   (C) Calm
   (D) Stall
   Answer: (C) Calm

3. We heard the news from a reliable source.
   (A) Real
   (B) Trustworthy
   (C) Disputed
   (D) Dubious
   Answer: (B) Trustworthy

4. The vigorous exercises left the old man exhausted.
   (A) Excited
   (B) Inactive
   (C) Ill
   (D) Extremely tired
   Answer: (D) Extremely tired

5. The speech was so long that the audience were nodding off.
   (A) Falling asleep
   (B) Shaking their heads
   (C) Agreeing
   (D) Clapping
   Answer: (A) Falling asleep

6. Such behavior is totally unexpected from someone of his calibre.
   (A) Low quality
   (B) Measure
   (C) Size
   (D) Competence
   Answer: (D) Competence

7. He's not afraid to say what he thinks. His candour is refreshing.
   (A) Frankness
   (B) Humour
   (C) Energy
   (D) Enthusiasm
   Answer: (A) Frankness

8. To be a good writer, one needs to be observant of life and people.
   (A) Unaware
   (B) Accepting
   (C) Heedless
   (D) Take careful notice
   Answer: (D) Take careful notice

9. The policemen fired into the air to disperse the crowd.
   (A) To frighten
   (B) To threaten
   (C) To break up
   (D) To gather
   Answer: (C) To break up

10. The late Queen was a woman of great depth and discernment.
    (A) Shrewdness
    (B) Manners
    (C) Fashionable
    (D) Wealthy
    Answer: (A) Shrewdness

Directions (Q. Nos. 46-50): Each of the following sentences is divided into 3 (three) parts which are marked (A), (B), (C). One of these parts may contain an error or may not be acceptable in standard written communication. Mark that part as your answer. If there is no error, mark your answer as (D).

46. Children should be / teach / to share things with others. / No error
    (A) Children should be
    (B) teach (Error: Should be 'taught')
    (C) to share things with others.
    (D) No error
    Answer: (B)

47. It's okay / to disagree / but one should do it respectively. / No error
    (A) It's okay
    (B) to disagree
    (C) but one should do it respectively. (Error: Should be 'respectfully')
    (D) No error
    Answer: (C)

48. We had / a great time / in the movies. / No error
    (A) We had
    (B) a great time
    (C) in the movies. (Error: Should be 'at the movies')
    (D) No error
    Answer: (C)

49. Charles Dickens / is / a great writer during his time. / No error
    (A) Charles Dickens
    (B) is
    (C) a great writer during his time. (Error: Should be 'was' a great writer, or 'is a great writer' (present tense) with a different phrasing)
    (D) No error
    Answer: (C)

50. True riches / is not to be found / but in good health. / No error
    (A) True riches
    (B) is not to be found (Error: Riches is plural, should be 'are not to be found')
    (C) but in good health.
    (D) No error
    Answer: (B)
