Directions (Q. Nos. 31-40): In these questions, some of the sentences have errors and some have none. Find out which part of a sentence, A, B, C has an error and select that part as an answer. If there is no error, then D is the answer.

31. If your braces / is loose your / trousers come down. / No error
(A) If your braces
(B) is loose your
(C) trousers come down.
(D) No error
Answer: (B) is loose your (Error: The verb 'is' should be 'are' because 'braces' is a plural noun.)

32. This misogynist hates / all mother-in-laws / and house-maids / No error
(A) This misogynist hates
(B) all mother-in-laws
(C) and house-maids
(D) No error
Answer: (B) all mother-in-laws (Error: The plural of 'mother-in-law' is 'mothers-in-law'.)

33. He hasn't a / beautiful furniture / in his house. / No error
(A) He hasn't a
(B) beautiful furniture
(C) in his house.
(D) No error
Answer: (A) He hasn't a (Error: 'Furniture' is an uncountable noun. It should be "He hasn't **any** beautiful furniture" or "He hasn't a **piece of** beautiful furniture".)

34. I believe / he is more wicked / than any living man. / No error
(A) I believe
(B) he is more wicked
(C) than any living man.
(D) No error
Answer: (C) than any living man. (Error: The correct comparison for 'any' in a superlative context should be 'than any **other** living man' to exclude himself from the comparison set.)

35. They have / a little money / so they're not poor. / No error
(A) They have
(B) a little money
(C) so they're not poor.
(D) No error
Answer: (D) No error ('A little money' means some money, which is enough to support the conclusion 'so they're not poor'.)

36. Is the old / always wiser / than the young? / No error
(A) Is the old
(B) always wiser
(C) than the young?
(D) No error
Answer: (D) No error ('The old' and 'the young' correctly refer to groups of people.)

37. What nonsense / to have a picnic / in such a bad weather! / No error
(A) What nonsense
(B) to have a picnic
(C) in such a bad weather!
(D) No error
Answer: (C) in such a bad weather! (Error: 'Weather' is an uncountable noun. It should be "in such bad weather" or "in such a bad **time**.")

38. That is the girl / whom we all agree / is very beautiful. / No error
(A) That is the girl
(B) whom we all agree
(C) is very beautiful.
(D) No error
Answer: (B) whom we all agree (Error: The relative pronoun should be 'who' because it is the subject of the verb 'is' in the clause "who is very beautiful". The clause "we all agree" is parenthetical.)

39. He was so afraid / that his knees / knocked one another. / No error
(A) He was so afraid
(B) that his knees
(C) knocked one another.
(D) No error
Answer: (C) knocked one another. (Error: When referring to two items, the phrase should be 'knocked **each other**'. 'One another' is typically used for more than two.)

40. There wasn't much furniture / in the room-just / a table and, few chairs. / No error
(A) There wasn't much furniture
(B) in the room-just
(C) a table and, few chairs.
(D) No error
Answer: (C) a table and, few chairs. (Error: 'Few chairs' means almost none. It should be 'a few chairs' (meaning some chairs) to logically follow 'just'.)
