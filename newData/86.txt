Directions: In Questions 41-50 a part of the sentence is underlined. Below are given alternatives to the underlined part in a, b and c. Choose the correct alternative. In case no improvement is needed your answer is d.

41. Japanese armies **effort to destroy** the allied forces at Imphal and invade India, but were driven back into Burma.
    a) attempted to destroy, b) experimented to destroy, c) strengthened to destruct, d) No improvement.
    Answer: a) attempted to destroy

42. He stopped **to work** an hour ago.
    a) worked, b) to have worked, c) working, d) No improvement.
    Answer: c) working

43. My teacher is the **kinder of all**.
    a) kind, b) kindly, c) kindest, d) No improvement.
    Answer: c) kindest

44. The accident occurred in the **centre** of the road.
    a) middle, b) path, c) way, d) No improvement.
    Answer: a) middle

45. Hardly had I reached the station **when** the train started.
    a) then, b) than, c) since, d) No improvement.
    Answer: d) No improvement (The pair is 'Hardly...when')

46. He is **wilful** to help you.
    a) willingly, b) willing, c) wilfully, d) No improvement.
    Answer: b) willing

47. His father **died** when he was very young.
    a) broke down, b) passed away, c) took off, d) No improvement.
    Answer: b) passed away (often used as a gentler euphemism)

48. She could not help **laughing** at his foolishness.
    a) to laugh, b) laughed, c) by laughing, d) No improvement.
    Answer: d) No improvement (The construction 'could not help' is followed by a gerund)

49. They **could walk** to school because it was close.
    a) start walking, b) walking, c) were walked, d) No improvement.
    Answer: d) No improvement (Use of 'could' indicates ability, which is appropriate here)

50. He is one of the bright **student** of his class.
    a) brightest students, b) brighter student, c) brightly students, d) No improvement.
    Answer: a) brightest students (The construction 'one of the' requires a superlative adjective followed by a plural noun)
