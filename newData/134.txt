*** SECTION-II: ERROR CORRECTION/SENTENCE STRUCTURE (Q. Nos. 6-10) ***

Directions (Q. Nos. 6-10): In these questions, some of the sentences have errors and some have none. Find out which part of a sentence (A), (B), (C) has an error and select that part as the answer. If there is no error, then (D) is the answer.

6. She promised to / accompany **with** me / to the park / No error.
 (A) She promised to
 (B) accompany with me
 (C) to the park
 (D) No error.
[Answer: (B) accompany with me] (Error: 'accompany' is a transitive verb and doesn't take 'with' when the object is a person. Correct: accompany me)

7. They that **hanker** / after fame and money / are likely to be disappointed / No error.
 (A) They that hanker
 (B) after fame and money
 (C) are likely to be disappointed
 (D) No error.
[Answer: (D) No error.]

8. You should make it a rule / never to interfere with other **affair** / that does not concern you / No error.
 (A) You should make it a rule
 (B) never to interfere with other affair
 (C) that does not concern you
 (D) No error.
[Answer: (B) never to interfere with other affair] (Error: Should be 'other **people's** affair' or 'other **affairs**')

9. He is **so** generous / that he will not hesitate / to lend you any amount / No error.
 (A) He is so generous
 (B) that he will not hesitate
 (C) to lend you any amount
 (D) No error.
[Answer: (D) No error.]

10. The children **were** / running all over / and making a lot of noise / No error.
 (A) The children were
 (B) running all over
 (C) and making a lot of noise
 (D) No error.
[Answer: (D) No error.]
