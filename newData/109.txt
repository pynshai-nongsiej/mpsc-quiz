Directions: In the following sentences, find out which part of a sentence has an error and mark the correct alternative under the letter a, b or c. Mark the letter 'd' if the sentence is fully correct and has no error.

16. The girl who you wanted (a) / to marry is a teacher (b)/in a convent school (c)/ No error (d).
Error in: (a) (Answer - *Should be 'whom' instead of 'who'*)

17. She had her bath, (a) / ate her breakfast (b)/and after all went to school (c)/No error (d).
Error in: (c) (Answer - *The correct phrase is 'after that' or remove 'after all'*)

18. The ship's crew has (a) / tried to save the passengers (b)/from drowning in water (c) / No error (d).
Error in: (a) (Answer - *'Crew' is often treated as plural, so 'have' is preferred, or 'crew members have'. Also, 'drowning' implies in water, so (c) is potentially redundant but (a) is a more definite error*)
*Selecting (a) as the intended error for subject-verb agreement.*

19. This matter should be (a)/ discussed strictly between (b)/ you and I (c) / No error (d).
Error in: (c) (Answer - *Should be 'you and me' as 'between' takes objective case*)

20. The audience of a football match (a) / are pleased (b) / when a goal is scored (c) / No error (d).
Error in: (b) (Answer - *'Audience' is singular here, so 'is pleased' is correct, making 'are pleased' an error*)
